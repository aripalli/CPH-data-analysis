Interactive visualization

The greatest value of a picture is when it forces us to notice what we never expected to see.

This map is awesome! It shows you a lot about the different districts in Copenhagen! It has a lot of features. Some of them are listed below. Go explore and learn more about Copenhagen and its districts.

  • Click on a district to zoom and learn more about it
  • The bars to the right show income and age distribution
  • The slider lets you explore different years
  • The buttons on top colors the districts based on percent danes or percent with high income
  • Hover over a district to get some basic information about it

Below are some noteworthy areas found in the map and some guesses on why the statistics look like they do.

Egmonts

Click to see Egmonts on the map

If you move the timeline slider from 2008 to 2013 it can be seen that some areas are always red/orange. One of them is Egmonts. The initial thought was it has probably a low percentage of danes living in this area. However we can see there is about 89% danes living in this area. But as seen from the stacked age bar there are almost only people of 18 - 29 years living there. That is interesting! If we go to the google maps to the right we see that the only homes to be found in this area are college campus dorms. That explaines the low percentage of wealthy people in that area, since all of them are students.

Nørre Alles

Click to see Nørre Alles on the map

The same can be seen for the area Nørre Alles, which is next to Egmonts. It has a low percentage of wealthy residents, however there are no dorms there. But if you click on the area you can see the age distribution. A large portion of the people are above 65 years old. If we look closer into this area we have found out that there is a home for the elderly, De Gamles By, occupying the area. We also notice from the income bar that the majority of the people in Nørre Alles have medium income compared to the college dorm which consisted mostly of low income people. A similiar area with an elder home is Sundparken (click to see on map).

Gavlhus

Click to see Gavlhus on the map

Other areas that have low percentage of high income residents over all years include Gavlhus and Tingbjergs. Tingbjergs is next to Gavlhus. If you click on one of these distrtics, you can see that the age distribution is not abnormal as the areas metnioned above. However if you hover over the district or click the "Precentage of Danes button" you find out that this area has almost 75% foreigners living there. Is that perhaps the reason?
